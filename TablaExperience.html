My 12-Year Tabla Journey

Aarya Patel - [BA Graduate in Tabla], has been learning Tabla from 2008 to 2020. It has been an enriching and transformative experience for me. At the age of 4, I embarked on this musical journey with fascination and curiosity. Initially guided by my guru, I delved into the fundamentals of Tabla, mastering basic strokes and rhythms that form the backbone of Hindustani classical music. As the years progressed, my passion for Tabla deepened. I explored various taals (rhythmic cycles) and compositions, learning from renowned maestros who imparted their wisdom and unique styles. My guru brought new insights, shaping not only my technical proficiency but also my understanding of the cultural and spiritual dimensions of Tabla music.

By my teenage years, I began to perform publicly, initially in local gatherings and later on larger stages. These performances were not just opportunities to showcase my skills but also moments of connection with audiences who shared my love for Indian classical music. Collaborations with other musicians introduced me to fusion genres, blending traditional Tabla rhythms with contemporary sounds. Throughout this journey, discipline and perseverance were my constant companions. Daily practice sessions became rituals of self-improvement, honing my ability to maintain intricate rhythms and improvise creatively. Beyond technical prowess, Tabla taught me patience and resilience, virtues that extended beyond music into all aspects of my life.

As I entered my twenties, my commitment to Tabla remained strong. However, in 2020, I made the difficult decision to step away temporarily from active playing due to other commitments and personal priorities. Despite this pause, my connection with Tabla continues to influence my appreciation for music and the arts. Looking ahead, I am eager to resume my Tabla journey, seeking new challenges and opportunities to grow as a musician. My 12-year odyssey with Tabla has been not only a musical pursuit but a transformative life experience, shaping my identity and inspiring me to share the beauty of Tabla with others.
